# Scoring Rubric - Post Evaluator

Detailed rubric for each of the 5 evaluation lenses. Use this to understand what each score range means and how to improve.

## Scoring Scale

| Range | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 90-100 | Exceptional. Publish-ready, no changes needed for this lens. |
| 80-89 | Strong. Minor polish possible but not required. |
| 70-79 | Acceptable. Passes threshold for most lenses but has room for improvement. |
| 60-69 | Below threshold for most lenses. Specific issues need fixing. |
| 50-59 | Weak. Significant rewrite needed for this lens. |
| 0-49 | Fundamental problems. Likely needs a complete rethink of this aspect. |

Thresholds vary by lens because some qualities matter more than others. Voice (75) is held to a higher standard than Hook (65) because a post with a weak opening can still deliver value, but a post that sounds like AI wrote it undermines trust.

---

## 1. CLARITY (Threshold: 70)

**Core question:** Can a busy person get the point in 10 seconds?

### 90-100: Crystal clear
- One idea, stated plainly, impossible to misunderstand
- Every sentence earns its place - nothing redundant
- Structure guides the eye (scannable without reading every word)
- A reader could summarise the post in one sentence after skimming

**Example of a 95:** "You're spending 3 hours on reports. This script does it in 3 minutes. Here's how to set it up."

### 70-79: Acceptable clarity
- Main idea is clear but buried slightly
- Some sentences could be cut without losing meaning
- Structure is mostly scannable
- Reader gets it, but has to work a little

### Below 70: Fails
- Multiple ideas competing for attention
- Jargon or technical terms without context
- Wall-of-text formatting
- Reader finishes and thinks "what was the point?"

**Example of a 45:** A 600-word post that covers three different topics, uses industry acronyms without explanation, and has no visual breaks or structure.

### Common fixes for low Clarity:
- Cut the post down to ONE main idea
- Delete any sentence that doesn't directly support that idea
- Add line breaks and white space
- Replace jargon with plain language (or explain it in parentheses)
- Front-load the key message - don't make readers hunt for it

## 2. VOICE (Threshold: 75)

**Core question:** Does this sound like a real person wrote it, or does it sound like AI?

### 90-100: Authentic and distinctive
- Reads like someone talking to you over coffee
- Natural rhythm with short punchy sentences mixed with longer ones
- Uses "you" language throughout
- Has personality - you could identify the author without a byline
- Contractions used naturally (I'm, can't, you're)

**Example of a 95:** "Look, I tested this on 12 accounts last week. Nine of them saw cost per lead drop by 20%+. The other three? Already running lean - there wasn't much fat to cut."

### 70-79: Acceptable voice
- Generally conversational but with occasional stiff phrasing
- Some sentences feel written rather than spoken
- "You" language present but inconsistent
- Personality shows through in places

### Below 75: Fails
- Reads like a corporate blog or press release
- Formal academic tone
- Contains AI-isms (banned phrases, generic filler)
- Passive voice dominates
- Could have been written by anyone (or anything)

**Example of a 40:** "In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape, it is essential for marketing professionals to leverage cutting-edge automation solutions to streamline their workflow and drive meaningful results."

### Common fixes for low Voice:
- Read the post out loud - if you wouldn't say it, rewrite it
- Replace passive voice with active ("was implemented" -> "I set this up")
- Add contractions where natural
- Cut any sentence that sounds like it belongs in a whitepaper
- Remove all banned AI-ism phrases (see SKILL.md for the full list)
- Write to ONE person using "you", not to a crowd

## 3. HOOK (Threshold: 65)

**Core question:** Would you stop scrolling after reading the first 2 lines?

### 90-100: Irresistible opening
- First line creates immediate tension, curiosity, or recognition
- Opens with a problem, surprising fact, or bold claim
- You genuinely want to know what comes next
- No preamble, no context-setting, no warm-up

**Example of a 95:** "I deleted 40% of our Google Ads keywords yesterday. Conversions went up."

### 65-79: Decent hook
- Opening is interesting but not surprising
- Gets to the point within the first 3-4 lines
- Some context-setting but not excessive
- Reader continues but isn't pulled forward urgently

### Below 65: Fails
- Opens with background or context before getting to the point
- First line is generic or predictable
- Starts with "I've been thinking about..." or similar warm-up
- Reader can predict where the post is going from the first line

**Example of a 30:** "As we move into 2025, I've been reflecting on some of the changes in the digital marketing industry and wanted to share some thoughts on what I think will matter most."

### Common fixes for low Hook:
- Delete the first paragraph and start with what's currently in paragraph 2 or 3
- Lead with a result, number, or surprising outcome
- Open with the reader's problem, not your journey
- Try the "delete until interesting" test - keep cutting from the top until the opening grabs you
- Ask: "Would I stop scrolling for this?" If no, rewrite.

## 4. SUBSTANCE (Threshold: 70)

**Core question:** Would a skeptical expert nod or roll their eyes?

### 90-100: Expert-level substance
- Concrete, specific takeaway the reader can act on today
- Shows rather than tells (examples, numbers, screenshots, before/after)
- Demonstrates first-hand experience, not regurgitated advice
- A skeptical practitioner would learn something or validate their thinking

**Example of a 95:** A post that walks through a specific script setup, shows the before/after data from a real account, and explains exactly why the numbers changed.

### 70-79: Acceptable substance
- Has a clear takeaway but could be more specific
- Some concrete examples but also some generic advice
- Reader gets value but might not change behaviour
- Expert wouldn't disagree but also wouldn't bookmark it

### Below 70: Fails
- Vague advice anyone could give ("focus on what matters")
- All tell, no show - claims without evidence
- Theoretical without practical application
- A reader finishes and hasn't learned anything actionable

**Example of a 35:** "AI is going to change everything. You need to start using it now or you'll fall behind. The key is to experiment and find what works for your business."

### Common fixes for low Substance:
- Add at least one specific example, number, or result
- Replace vague claims with concrete evidence ("improved results" -> "CTR went from 2.1% to 4.8%")
- Include a step the reader can take immediately after reading
- Share what you actually did, not what someone should do in theory
- If you can't point to real data or experience, the post isn't ready

## 5. PLATFORM FIT (Threshold: 70)

**Core question:** Does the formatting match what works on this specific platform?

### LinkedIn (90-100)
- ~50 lines, never over 80
- Generous white space and line breaks
- Zero hashtags
- 0-2 emojis maximum
- Uses arrows or bold for structure
- Reads well on mobile (short lines)

### LinkedIn (Below 70)
- Over 80 lines or dense walls of text
- Hashtags present
- More than 2 emojis
- No visual breaks
- Formatting that doesn't render on mobile

### Circle (90-100)
- **Bold** headings only (no # markdown headings)
- 2 blank lines before headings
- Standard bullets (- or *)
- Appropriate depth for a practitioner community
- Clean, consistent spacing

### Circle (Below 70)
- Uses # markdown headings (won't render correctly)
- Inconsistent spacing
- Arrow bullets instead of standard
- Too shallow or too long for the community context

### Blog (90-100)
- Has # title and ## section headings
- Good intro/body/conclusion structure
- Appropriate length for the topic
- Well-organized with clear flow

### Blog (Below 70)
- No heading structure
- Missing title
- No clear sections or flow
- Either way too short or padded with filler

### Common fixes for low Platform Fit:
- **LinkedIn:** Cut length, remove hashtags, add line breaks, remove excess emojis
- **Circle:** Replace # headings with **bold**, add double blank lines before headings, use - bullets
- **Blog:** Add # title and ## sections, structure with intro/body/conclusion

## How Scores Combine

The overall score is the average of all 5 lens scores, with penalties for deterministic issues:
- Each banned phrase found: -15 points
- Each style warning (em-dash, etc.): -3 points

A post **passes** when:
1. No deterministic errors (banned phrases, formatting errors)
2. No lens scores below its threshold
3. Overall score >= 70

A post **fails** if ANY of these conditions are not met. The failing lenses return specific `rewriteGuidance` to tell you exactly what to fix.

## Improvement Loop Rules

When rewriting to fix failed lenses:
- **Only fix the failing lenses.** If Clarity scores 85, don't touch structure.
- **Use the rewriteGuidance.** Each failed lens provides specific instructions.
- **Max 3 iterations.** If it still fails after 3 rewrites, show the best version.
- **Don't introduce new problems.** Fixing voice shouldn't break clarity.
