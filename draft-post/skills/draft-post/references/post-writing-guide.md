# Post Writing Guide

Guidelines for writing LinkedIn, X/Twitter, and community posts that sound human and drive engagement.

## File Naming Convention

**Format:** `[platform]-YYYYMMDD-descriptive-title.md`

**Examples:**
- `linkedin-20260311-automation-lessons.md`
- `x-20260311-automation-lessons.md`

**Platform prefixes:**
- `linkedin-` for LinkedIn posts
- `x-` for X/Twitter posts
- `email-` for email content

## Core Writing Rules

### Write to One Person
- Write as if speaking to a single reader
- Use "you" not "you all." Never "some of you"
- Imagine a specific person reading this. What do they need to hear?

### Lead With the Point
- Open with the insight, result, or problem
- No preamble, no throat-clearing, no "let me set the scene"
- The first line decides if someone keeps reading. Make it count.

### Be Specific
- "I cut reporting time from 3 hours to 12 minutes" beats "I saved a lot of time"
- Numbers, names, timeframes, outcomes
- Vague posts get vague engagement

### One Idea Per Post
- If you catch yourself writing "also" or "another thing," stop
- That's a second post. Save it for tomorrow.

### Earn Every Sentence
- Read each sentence and ask: does this add something?
- If not, cut it. Shorter is almost always better.
- White space is your friend. Let ideas breathe.

## Patterns to Avoid

### AI-isms (these scream "written by AI")
- "Then I hit a wall" - dramatic narrative transitions
- "Here's what changed everything" - overhyped revelations
- "The irony?" - rhetorical questions as transitions
- "But here's the thing:" - formulaic setup lines
- "Let me explain" / "Let me break this down"
- "Game-changer" / "changed the game"
- "Here's why this matters"
- "The secret is..." / "The key is..."
- "Here's what most people miss"
- "Here's what most people don't realise"
- Any sentence that sounds like a YouTube video title

### Structural Cliches
- "It's not about X, it's about Y" - overused framing
- "Most people do X. The best do Y." - lazy contrast
- "Stop doing X. Start doing Y." - preachy
- Setup paragraphs that don't say anything new
- Lists of 10+ items (nobody reads past 5)

### Tone Problems
- Em dashes for dramatic pauses (use commas or periods instead)
- Excessive exclamation marks
- Corporate speak ("leverage," "synergy," "utilise," "streamline")
- Unnecessary superlatives ("incredible," "amazing," "revolutionary")
- Writing to a crowd instead of one person

## Platform-Specific Formatting

### LinkedIn Posts

**Formatting:**
- Use separators between sections: `---`
- Use arrows for bullet points: `->`
- Break up text with white space (short paragraphs, 1-3 sentences each)
- Keep to ~50 lines of text. 80 lines absolute max.
- One thought per paragraph

**Style:**
- No hashtags
- Limited or no emojis
- Direct and punchy
- Lead with the insight or result
- End with a question or clear CTA to drive comments

**Algorithm context (2025-2026):**
LinkedIn uses semantic matching. Posts get shown to people predicted to find them relevant.

*What wins:*
- Attention/dwell time (people slow down to read)
- Comment depth (replies-to-replies)
- Semantic consistency (stay in your lane)
- Practitioner voice (lived specifics, not polished AI-speak)

*High-attention post types:*
1. Contrarian take + proof
2. Tactical checklist/SOP
3. Case teardown with numbers
4. Framework (named, structured)
5. Before/After transformation

### X/Twitter Posts

**Single post:**
- 280 characters max
- Punchy, direct, no filler
- One clear idea
- No hashtags unless specifically part of a conversation

**Thread format:**
- First post is the hook. It must work completely on its own.
- Number posts (1/N format or just sequential)
- Each post should make sense independently
- End with a summary or CTA
- 5-10 posts is the sweet spot. More than 15 and you're losing people.

**Style:**
- Conversational, not formal
- Short sentences
- Contractions are fine
- Hot takes work if you can back them up
- Questions drive engagement

### Community Posts (Circle, Discourse, Slack, etc.)

**Formatting:**
- **Don't** use Markdown heading syntax (`#`, `##`, etc.) unless the platform supports it
- **Do** use bold text with extra spacing: `**Heading Text**`
- Add two blank lines before each section heading
- One blank line after headings before content
- Standard bullets (`-` or `*`)
- Keep formatting simple. Most community platforms have limited Markdown support.

**Style:**
- More personal, more context
- You're talking to peers, not an audience
- Share what you learned, not what you know
- Ask for input. Communities are conversations, not broadcasts.

### Email Posts

**Only create when specifically asked.**

**Formatting:**
- Standard markdown
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
- Clear subject line
- Include a clear CTA
- PS lines work well for secondary asks

## Post Structure Patterns

### The Insight Lead
```
[Surprising statement or result]

[Context: what you did and why]

[What happened / what you learned]

[What this means for the reader]

[CTA or question]
```

### The Problem-Solution
```
[Problem the reader recognises]

[Why common solutions don't work]

[What actually works (specific)]

[Proof / example]

[Takeaway]
```

### The Before-After
```
[Before state: specific, relatable pain]

[What changed (the action, tool, mindset shift)]

[After state: specific, measurable improvement]

[How the reader can do the same]
```

### The Contrarian Take
```
[Popular belief or common advice]

[Why it's wrong or incomplete]

[What you've found instead (with evidence)]

[The nuance / when the popular belief IS right]

[Takeaway]
```

### The Tactical How-To
```
[Result the reader wants]

[Step 1: specific action]
[Step 2: specific action]
[Step 3: specific action]

[Common mistake to avoid]

[Expected outcome]
```

## Hook Formulas That Work

Strong openings that stop the scroll:

- **The number hook**: "I tested 47 headlines. Only 3 patterns consistently worked."
- **The confession**: "I've been doing this wrong for 5 years."
- **The result**: "Cut our reporting time from 3 hours to 12 minutes."
- **The question**: "What if the bottleneck isn't your tool, it's your process?"
- **The contrarian**: "The best marketers I know don't track ROAS."
- **The observation**: "Every agency I've audited this year has the same blind spot."
- **The timestamp**: "Last Tuesday I automated something that used to take my whole morning."

Weak openings to avoid:
- "I want to talk about..." (nobody cares what you want)
- "In today's fast-paced world..." (cliche)
- "Have you ever wondered..." (overused)
- "Let me share..." (just share it)
- "I'm excited to announce..." (your excitement isn't a hook)

## CTA Patterns

End with something the reader can act on:

- **The question**: "What's the one thing you'd automate first?"
- **The challenge**: "Try this for one week. See what happens."
- **The resource**: "I wrote up the full process [link]."
- **The invitation**: "If you've done something similar, I'd love to hear what worked."
- **The next step**: "Start with step 1. The rest follows."

Avoid:
- "Like and share if you agree" (desperate)
- "Follow me for more" (on LinkedIn especially, this is tired)
- "Thoughts?" (lazy. Ask a specific question.)

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing any post, verify:
- [ ] Hook stops the scroll (would YOU stop for this?)
- [ ] Written to one person ("you"), not a crowd
- [ ] Specific examples, numbers, or details included
- [ ] No AI-isms or corporate speak
- [ ] No em dashes
- [ ] Every sentence earns its place
- [ ] Platform formatting is correct
- [ ] Clear takeaway or CTA
- [ ] Reads like a real person, not a marketing department
- [ ] One idea, not three ideas crammed together
